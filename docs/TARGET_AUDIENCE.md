# Target Audience

## Primary audiences

### 1. Serious golf bettors

These users allocate bankroll weekly and need quick, explainable comparisons across outrights, placements, and matchup-style markets.

### 2. DFS and fantasy golf players

These users need projections, leverage context, course fit, and workflow speed under time pressure.

### 3. Research-oriented golf fans

These users want readable analytics and transparent explanations without needing to become full-time quant users.

## Internal audiences

### 4. Editors

Editors need structured blocks, templates, metadata controls, AI assistance with grounding, and approval visibility.

### 5. Analysts

Analysts need source lineage, model outputs, performance tracking, and comparison tools.

### 6. Admins and operators

Admins need secure control over users, roles, subscriptions, provider health, and audit review.

## Audience priorities

The product should prioritize audiences that create the strongest loop between acquisition, conversion, and retention:

1. serious golf bettors
2. time-constrained weekly premium users
3. DFS and fantasy users
4. research-oriented golf fans
5. internal editorial and operational teams

## Audience design implications

- discovery experiences must be indexable and readable
- premium experiences must reward repeat weekly use
- internal tools must preserve trust and accountability at scale
