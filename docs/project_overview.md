# Project Overview

## Purpose

Caddy Stats is a production-grade golf analytics platform that unifies structured golf data, projections, betting intelligence, editorial publishing, and premium subscriber experiences under the Strik3Zone brand.

## Platform summary

Caddy Stats exists to:

- centralize golf research that is normally split across sportsbooks, stat tools, spreadsheets, and content sites
- expose explainable analytics instead of opaque rankings or generic picks
- support SEO and editorial growth without inventing data or unsupported claims
- create recurring subscription value from differentiated workflows and trusted intelligence

## Core product pillars

1. **Stats infrastructure** for players, tournaments, courses, rounds, projections, and market context.
2. **Projection intelligence** with model lineage, freshness, and reviewable methodology.
3. **Editorial publishing** with structured content, templates, and data-grounded workflows.
4. **Research tooling** for betting, fantasy, and tournament preparation.
5. **Monetization systems** for subscriptions, affiliate flows, and retention.
6. **Governed AI assistance** for drafting, summarization, and model review.

## Scope

### In scope

- PGA-focused player, tournament, and course analytics
- public SEO pages and premium subscriber surfaces
- editorial and admin workflows
- API-driven frontend and operational tooling
- PostgreSQL-backed stats, content, entitlement, and audit records
- AI assistance with grounding and human review controls

### Out of scope for the current foundation

- multi-sport expansion
- autonomous bet placement
- unsupported real-time claims when source data is stale
- community/social features as a first-order platform requirement

## Product and business intent

The platform should turn public discovery into repeatable member and subscriber behavior by pairing trustworthy research with useful weekly workflows. Revenue should come from subscription value, affiliate leverage, and long-term data differentiation rather than hype-driven content.

## Delivery constraints

- PostgreSQL remains the source of truth.
- Build order starts with documentation and governance.
- Architectural changes require explicit decision capture.
- Public and premium claims must remain grounded in verified or derived data.
