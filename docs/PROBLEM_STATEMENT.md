# Problem Statement

## Core problem

Golf bettors, fantasy players, analysts, and golf media readers often rely on disconnected sources to make weekly decisions. Stats live in one place, odds in another, projections in spreadsheets, and editorial narratives somewhere else.

## Why that is a problem

This fragmentation creates repeated failure modes:

- decision workflows are slow and hard to repeat each week
- recommendations are hard to verify or explain
- editorial content drifts away from source data
- premium products look interchangeable with free alternatives
- internal publishing and review processes become manual and opaque

## Market gap

The market has plenty of golf content and raw data, but fewer products that combine:

- verified golf statistics
- explainable projections
- fast comparison workflows
- structured editorial systems
- clear monetization boundaries
- auditable AI assistance

## What Caddy Stats must solve

Caddy Stats must reduce fragmentation by creating one platform where verified data, projections, editorial output, and premium decision workflows reinforce each other instead of living in separate tools.

## Product implication

The platform cannot win by publishing generic picks or commodity dashboards. It must win through trust, workflow speed, explainability, and differentiated weekly value.
